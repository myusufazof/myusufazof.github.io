About

Building a research identity across molecular neuroscience, neurodegeneration, and translational biotechnology.

My work centers on how neuronal signaling systems shape sensory function and disease-relevant communication. At the Johns Hopkins School of Medicine, I study neuronal membrane proteasome-dependent signaling in primary mouse dorsal root ganglion neurons using functional calcium imaging and quantitative analysis.

I am also interested in antibody-based therapeutics, blood-brain barrier delivery, biomarker development, and early translational strategies for neurodegenerative diseases including Alzheimer’s disease.
